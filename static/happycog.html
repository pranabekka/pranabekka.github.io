Font selection

I decided to forgo the helvetica-ish font, since it's not that great for a lot of reading, and I instead chose Atkinson Hyperlegible, which I've paired with DM Serif Display for the headings.

The cool thing about Atkinson Hyperlegible, is that it's designed for low-vision people, in collaboration with the Braille Institute.

DM Serif is just there to look nice.

LongWordsCanAlsoBreakThings

Well, that's not really a long word, but imagine something like "incomprehensible" in your main title. Or supercalifragilisticexpialidocious anywhere in your text.

You should ideally use soft-hyphen entities (&shy), but that's not always possible, or you might forget, so an automatic solution should be built into your stylesheet.

The hyphens: auto property might help, but it'll hyphenate words even if they could just be put onto a new line to make the whole word easier to read. Instead, I use the overflow-wrap: break-word property, because it only wraps words if they can't fit on a line by themselves.
