NOTE: Pay attention to your instructor, and don’t try to do more than they tell you. Reading is not the same as practice.

Use the license application knowledge to get yourself a license at a much cheaper cost than doing it through a school, and use the driving knowledge to supplement your other lessons and understanding.

Driving🔗

There are 5 main things to concern yourself with in the beginning: At your hands is the steering wheel, at your left is the gear shift, and at your feet are the accelerator, brake and clutch.

gears:

  • 1: starting up
  • 2: 1-20 kmph
  • 3: 20-30 kmph
  • 4: 30-40 kmph
  • 5: 40-50+ kmph

why gears: rpm, stressing out the engine, inertia, gear sizes (small spins faster, cause less work per spin)

changing by feel: shuddering/struggling vs little/no resistance

Take-off🔗

Releasing the clutch creates some motion itself. Release it gently and press the accelerator slowly.

If you’re on a slope, facing up, hold the break, and release it slowly at the same time as the clutch. The moment the car seems steady, or even moving up a bit, switch your right foot from the break to the accelerator.

Take-off checklist:

  1. Adjust mirrors
  2. Side-view mirrors should show a third or less of the car, and mostly the road
  3. Wear seat belt
  4. Hold break pedal
  5. Release hand break
  6. Press the button and lower it
  7. Slowly release clutch (till the very end) and gently press accelerator (only a bit)

Stopping🔗

Put the gear in neutral before leaving the clutch and turning off the car. The gears should not be engaged unless you’re holding down the clutch and/or accelerator.
